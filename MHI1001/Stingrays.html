Stingray Facts

- Stingrays are able to breathe and collect oxygen through their gills which are found on the underside of their bodies.


- The holes on the top of their bodies, called spiracles allows them to breathe while they are buried under the sand.


- Since stingrays eyes are located on the top of their bodies and their mouths are on the bottom of their bodies,
they use a special sense called electroreception to detect where their food is in the water through special receptors
called ampullae of lorenzini.


- Electroreception allows the stingrays to sense electrical pluses in the water so they can
effectively hunt for their food in the wild.
